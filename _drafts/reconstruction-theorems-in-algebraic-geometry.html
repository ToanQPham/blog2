One question that is asked in algebraic geometry is

What geometric information can we obtain from a category $latex \mathcal{C}_X$ associated to the scheme $latex X$?

Possible choices for the category $latex \mathcal{C}_X$ in the question are

  • the category of coherent sheaves $latex \mathrm{coh}(X)$
  • the category of quasicoherent sheaves $latex \mathrm{Qcoh}(X)$
  • the bounded derived category of coherent sheaves $latex \mathbf{D}^{\mathrm{b}}(\mathrm{coh}(X))$
  • the derived category of quasicoherent sheaves $latex \mathbf{D}(\mathrm{Qcoh}(X))$
  • the subcategory of perfect complexes $latex \mathrm{Perf}(X)$

The question as stated originally is really broad, and in this post we will consider a specific instance of it.

Let $latex X$ and $latex Y$ be sufficiently schemes. Which conditions do we need to obtain that $latex X$ and $latex Y$ are isomorphic, given that $latex \mathcal{C}_X$ and $latex \mathcal{C}_Y$ are equivalent?

We will see that the type of equivalence in this question is important: we could ask this equivalence to be compatible with some extra structure on the category $latex \mathcal{C}_X$. These extra structures could be an abelian structure, a triangulated structure, a monoidal structure, a combination of both, ... The interplay of these choices will actually be really interesting!

The goal of this post is mainly to get some of the facts straight, and provide a short overview of the results. And it motivates one of the guiding principles in noncommutative algebraic geometry, originally due to Grothendieck:

To do geometry one does not need the space itself but only the category of sheaves on that would-be space.

By the way, if anyone can give me the exact location of this quote, or even the location of its attribution to Grothendieck by Manin, please tell me.

The reconstruction theorems

Gabriel's reconstruction theorem

The original reconstruction theorem goes back to the early sixties: in the PhD thesis by Pierre Gabriel it is proved in section VI.3 that if $latex X$ and $latex Y$ are noetherian schemes and $latex \mathrm{coh}(X)$ and $latex \mathrm{coh}(Y)$ are equivalent as abelian categories, then $latex X$ and $latex Y$ are isomorphic. So in this case we use the abelian structure of the category.
