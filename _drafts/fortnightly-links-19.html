Direct summand conjecture

I missed this for the previous fortnightly links, so to make up for this I'll write a little more than usual. The direct summand conjecture is one of the homological conjectures in commutative algebra and states the following:

Direct summand conjecture Let $f\colon R\to S$ be a morphism of noetherian rings such that $S$ is a finite $R$-module. If $R$ is regular, then $f$ splits as map of $R$-modules.

This conjecture has now been proved by Yves Andr√© in his preprint La conjecture du facteur direct using his other preprint Le lemme d'Abhyankar perfectoide.

There is also a preprint On the direct summand conjecture and its derived variant by Bhargav Bhatt which gives a shorter proof. In both cases the theory of perfectoid spaces is used, and unfortunately I cannot comment much on this. The derived invariant in Bhatt's title refers to a conjecture suggested by de Jong

Derived direct summand conjecture Left $f\colon X\to\operatorname{Spec} R$ be a proper surjective map. If $R$ is regular noetherian, then $R\to\mathbf{R}\Gamma(X,\mathcal{O}_X)$ splits in $\mathbf{D}(A)$.
