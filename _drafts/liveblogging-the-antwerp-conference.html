Reading my blog you could've figured out that we are having a big conference in Antwerp. Yesterday was too hectic to liveblog this, as the neverending author noticed, so I guess I'll just do one post every two days.

Monday September 19

A good talk should contain one joke and one proof, and you should be able to tell them apart.

Unfortunately I forgot who Matt attributed it to, I'll ask him. I think Ragnar turned the punchline into and they should not be the same, which works equally well.

On a more mathematical level, he describes Orlov's theorem as the most important result in algebra since Hilbert's syzygy theorem.
