I would like to make the analogy with bases and subspaces in vectorspaces (and explain how hard this analogy fails). The vectorspace in this case is a triangulated category, often from a geometric (i.e. $latex \mathbf{D}^{\mathrm{b}}(X)$ for some variety or scheme $latex X$) or algebraic (i.e. $latex \mathbf{D}^{\mathrm{b}}(A)$ for some algebra $latex A$) origin. We want to study this triangulated category, and understand how much of the geometry or algebra is reflected in the properties of this category.
